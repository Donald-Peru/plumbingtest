How to Fix a Leaking Pipe

Posted on November 2, 2025 by Plumbing Wiki Team

Leaking pipes are one of the most common plumbing problems homeowners face. The good news is, many small leaks can be temporarily managed with basic tools until a professional arrives.

Step 1: Locate the Source

Turn off your home’s main water supply before inspecting the pipe. Look closely around joints and fittings to identify exactly where the leak is coming from.

Step 2: Apply a Temporary Fix

Wrap the leaking area with plumber’s tape or use epoxy putty for a quick fix. These materials can stop the leak temporarily and prevent water damage until a replacement can be made.

“Quick fixes are helpful, but always replace damaged sections for a permanent solution.”

Step 3: Replace the Damaged Section

Use a pipe cutter to remove the damaged part. Replace it with a new section, applying sealant or Teflon tape to ensure watertight joints.

Pro Tip: Always keep spare couplings and a roll of plumber’s tape in your toolkit — they’re lifesavers during emergencies.
